Tourist visa

If you have a Tourist Visa worldwide, your spouse usually won't have the right to work in the respective country. Tourist visas are typically for leisure purposes, and employment is generally not permitted. Dependent children, however, may still have access to free education, subject to the rules of the country you're visiting.

Dependent visa

If you are holding a Work Visa worldwide, your spouse will have all the rights to live and work in the respective country. They will be entitled to pursue any job within the country. Additionally, your dependent children can access free education in that country.

Child visa

If your child has a visa for a particular country, their rights depend on the visa type. Family reunion or long-term residence visas usually allow children to live with their parents, but specific entitlements vary. Check the country's regulations for details.

Job seeker visa

If you hold a Job Seeker Visa worldwide, it typically allows you to stay in a country for a limited period to search for employment opportunities. During this time, you have the right to explore job options and attend interviews. The specific rights and duration can vary by country.

Global visa

A "Global Visa" is not a specific and universally recognized type of visa. Visa policies and categories vary widely among different countries, and there isn't a standard global visa that grants access to all nations. If you have a specific country or region in mind, it's best to inquire about the particular visa options and requirements for that location.

Family visa

With a Family Visa worldwide, your spouse typically has the right to live with you and may be allowed to work in the respective country. Dependent children may also be accommodated, often with access to education. Specific details can vary, so it's important to check the terms of your particular Family Visa and the regulations of the country you're in.

Employment visa

If you hold an Employment Visa worldwide, your spouse is often granted the right to live and work in the respective country. They usually have the freedom to pursue any job opportunity. Additionally, dependent children may be eligible for free education in the host country. Specific details may vary, so it's important to check the terms and conditions of the particular Employment Visa and the regulations of the country you're in.
