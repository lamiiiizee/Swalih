Schengen Visa Services

Ever dreamed of strolling down cobbled streets or falling in love under the Eiffel Tower like in your favourite movies? Europe's got that magic! Now that you're all in for a Euro trip, choosing where to go is the real head-scratcher. Each country's got its own charm, you know? But why pick one when you can have them all? Say hi to the Schengen visa! It's your pass to 27 awesome countries in Europe. No need to play favourites!

So, what's the deal with this visa?

Back in 1985, the Schengen visa came to life thanks to the Schengen agreement. Basically, it means you can roam freely through Europe without being hassled at borders. It's like the ultimate travel pass!

What can you do with it?
You can zip around most of Europe, except for a few countries.

Main Schengen Visa Types:

Uniform Schengen Visa (USV) - The Uniform Schengen Visa stands for a permit of one of the Schengen Area Member Countries to transit or reside in the desired territory for a certain period of time up to the maximum of 90 days every six-month period

Limited Territorial Validity Visa (LTV) - A visa that allows you to access a single Schengen country or specific Schengen countries, but not the entire Schengen area.

National Visa - A long-stay visa (Type D) allowing access to all Schengen country but to stay and work its only valid for particular at longer than 90 days.

Let's Discuss Uniform Schengen Visa!

Uniform Schengen Visas are of 2 categories â€“ Category A and Category C

  • Category A: Airport Transit Visa, allowing travel through the international zone of the Schengen country Airport without entering the Schengen Area.
  • Category C: Short-term visa, allowing residence in a Schengen Area for a certain period. This category includes Single-entry, Double-entry, and Multiple-entry visas.
Schengen Visa Types According to the Purpose of Travel:
  • Transit: Handy for layovers.
  • Tourist: Your golden ticket to explore Europe for up to 3 months. Just remember, no working for bucks on this one!
  • Visiting Family and Friends: Spend time with family and friends.
  • Business: For your work hustle in a Schengen country.
  • Work: If you're looking to clock in Euros and build a career there.
  • Study: Break boundaries with a Student Visa!
  • And many more...

Tourist visa

If you have a Tourist Visa worldwide, your spouse usually won't have the right to work in the respective country. Tourist visas are typically for leisure purposes, and employment is generally not permitted. Dependent children, however, may still have access to free education, subject to the rules of the country you're visiting.

Dependent visa

If you are holding a Work Visa worldwide, your spouse will have all the rights to live and work in the respective country. They will be entitled to pursue any job within the country. Additionally, your dependent children can access free education in that country.

Child visa

If your child has a visa for a particular country, their rights depend on the visa type. Family reunion or long-term residence visas usually allow children to live with their parents, but specific entitlements vary. Check the country's regulations for details.

Job seeker visa

If you hold a Job Seeker Visa worldwide, it typically allows you to stay in a country for a limited period to search for employment opportunities. During this time, you have the right to explore job options and attend interviews. The specific rights and duration can vary by country.

Global visa

A "Global Visa" is not a specific and universally recognized type of visa. Visa policies and categories vary widely among different countries, and there isn't a standard global visa that grants access to all nations. If you have a specific country or region in mind, it's best to inquire about the particular visa options and requirements for that location.

Family visa

With a Family Visa worldwide, your spouse typically has the right to live with you and may be allowed to work in the respective country. Dependent children may also be accommodated, often with access to education. Specific details can vary, so it's important to check the terms of your particular Family Visa and the regulations of the country you're in.

Employment visa

If you hold an Employment Visa worldwide, your spouse is often granted the right to live and work in the respective country. They usually have the freedom to pursue any job opportunity. Additionally, dependent children may be eligible for free education in the host country. Specific details may vary, so it's important to check the terms and conditions of the particular Employment Visa and the regulations of the country you're in.
